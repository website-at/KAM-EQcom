🎯 What is a KAM? — The Day 1 Briefing

▼

Listen — I've been in this game since Amazon India launched in 2013. I've trained over 500 KAMs. And the first thing I tell every single one of them is this:

KAM = Key Account Manager

You are the bridge between your Brand and the Platform. You own the P&L for that platform. Revenue, market share, profitability — that's your north star. You're not just "managing an account." You're running a business within a business.

Your North Star: Revenue Growth + Market Share + Profitability

Every morning when you open your laptop, three numbers should flash in your mind: How much did we sell yesterday? What's our share of the category? Are we making money doing it? That's it. Everything else is a means to these ends.

A Day in the Life of a KAM

9:00 AM — Dashboard Check: Open Seller Central / Vendor Central. Check yesterday's revenue, conversion rate, ad spend. Any stock-outs? Any negative reviews? Any account health alerts? This takes 20 minutes and sets your entire day's priorities.

10:00 AM — Stock & Supply Chain: Review fill rates. Are POs being fulfilled on time? Any ASN rejections? Any shortage claims from Amazon? Check inventory days of cover — if you're below 15 days on a hero SKU, that's a fire to fight NOW.

11:00 AM — Ad Performance: Review campaign dashboard. Which campaigns are bleeding money (ACOS > 35%)? Which keywords are converting? Pause losers, scale winners. This is where 20% of your effort drives 80% of your ad ROI.

1:00 PM — Platform Calls: Weekly sync with your Amazon/Flipkart Category Manager. Review performance, discuss upcoming deals, negotiate visibility placements. This relationship is GOLD — nurture it.

3:00 PM — Content & Catalog: Review listing quality scores. Are competitor listings better? Audit your top 10 ASINs for image quality, A+ content, keyword optimization. One listing fix can improve CVR by 15–20%.

5:00 PM — Reporting & Escalations: Update your weekly tracker. Flag any issues to leadership. Prepare data for tomorrow's WBR (Weekly Business Review).

The Weekly Rhythm

Weekly Business Review (WBR): This is your heartbeat. Every Monday or Tuesday, you present: last week's revenue vs target, stock-out incidents, ad efficiency metrics, competitive intel, and top 3 actions for this week. Keep it tight — 20 mins max.

Fill Rate Calls: With your supply chain team and 3PL partner. Are we shipping on time? What's the return rate? Any RTO hotspots?

Promotional Planning: What deals are running this week? Any Lightning Deals to submit? What's the competitive pricing landscape?

Monthly Deliverables

MBR (Monthly Business Review) Deck: Full P&L review — revenue, COGS, platform fees, ad spend, net margin. Category share trends. Competitive analysis. Next month's plan with targets and investments.

Forecast Submission: Next month's revenue forecast by SKU. This drives inventory planning, PO generation, and budget allocation. Get it wrong, and you either stock out (lose revenue) or overstock (kill your working capital).

The Yearly Calendar

A great KAM plans in quarters, not weeks. Here's what your year looks like:

Jan: Republic Day sales, New Year deals cleanup | Feb-Mar: Budget reset, new launches | Apr-Jun: EOSS (End of Season Sale), summer categories peak | Jul-Aug: Festive prep begins — 90 days before BBD/GIF | Sep-Oct: 🔥 BIG BILLION DAY / GREAT INDIAN FESTIVAL — this is your Super Bowl | Nov-Dec: Post-festive, winter categories, Christmas, New Year sales

A Platform Overview & Business Model

▼

I remember when Amazon India launched in June 2013. It started with just books and movies — and the skeptics said "Indians won't buy online." Fast forward to 2025, and Amazon India's estimated GMV crosses ₹3+ Lakh Crore. I've watched this platform evolve from a scrappy newcomer to the most sophisticated ecommerce ecosystem in the country.

How Amazon Makes Money

Understanding this is critical because these are your cost levers:

Referral Fees: 5–25% of selling price depending on category. Beauty & Personal Care sits at 5–12%. Electronics at 5–8%. Fashion at 15–22%. This is Amazon's core platform fee.

FBA Fees: Fulfillment by Amazon charges for pick, pack, ship + storage. Typically ₹25–80 per unit depending on weight/size + ₹10–30/unit/month for storage. During festive season, storage fees can spike 2x.

Advertising Revenue: Amazon's fastest growing P&L line. Sponsored Products, Brands, Display — this is where they're making serious margin. For you as a KAM, this means Amazon is INCENTIVIZED to push you to spend more on ads.

Subscription (Prime): Drives customer loyalty. Prime members convert 2–3x more than non-Prime. Your strategy must prioritize Prime-eligible products.

B KAM's Role on Amazon

▼

Vendor Central KAM Responsibilities

Managing POs (Purchase Orders): Every week, Amazon generates POs based on their demand forecast. Your job? Check PO accuracy (are quantities realistic?), ensure fill rate > 90%, and flag if a PO is for a discontinued SKU. I've seen brands lose ₹20L+ in shortage claims because nobody was checking POs daily.

ASN (Advanced Shipment Notice): Before you ship any PO, you must submit an ASN — it tells Amazon what's coming, when, and how many. Submit it wrong, and Amazon rejects the shipment. Submit it late, and you get dinged on fill rate. This is the single most operationally critical task in Vendor Central.

Shortage Claims: Amazon will claim they received fewer units than your ASN stated. Sometimes it's genuine, sometimes it's... questionable. You have a 30-day window to dispute. Always maintain your own dispatch records with photos, weight slips, and transporter receipts. I've recovered ₹15L+ in wrongful shortage claims for a single brand in one quarter.

Co-op Fees: Amazon charges Vendor Central brands a percentage of revenue as "trade terms." This includes damage allowance (1–3%), freight allowance (2–5%), and marketing co-op (1–3%). This eats into your margin BEFORE you even spend on ads. Factor this into your P&L from Day 1.

Seller Central KAM Responsibilities

Account Health Dashboard — check DAILY: Order Defect Rate (must be < 1%), Late Shipment Rate (< 4%), Cancellation Rate (< 2.5%). If any of these breach thresholds, Amazon can suppress your listings or suspend your account. I've seen it happen overnight with no warning.

FBA vs FBM Decision: FBA wins for speed and Buy Box advantage. But FBM can work for heavy/bulky items where FBA fees kill your margin. My 80/20 rule: put your top 20 SKUs (that drive 80% revenue) on FBA. Ship the rest yourself.

A-to-Z Claims: Customer complaint escalation. If a customer files an A-to-Z claim, respond within 24 hours with evidence. Win rate on well-documented responses is ~60%. Ignore it, and Amazon auto-refunds the customer from YOUR account.

Amazon's A9/A10 Search Algorithm

What drives organic rank on Amazon? In order of importance:

1. Sales Velocity (40% weight): Products that sell more rank higher. This is why launch-phase ad spend is critical — you need to generate sales velocity to start the flywheel.

2. Relevance (25% weight): How well your title, bullets, backend keywords match the search query. Do keyword research. Use all 250 backend keyword characters.

3. Conversion Rate (20% weight): Higher CVR = Amazon shows you more because you make them more money per impression. Optimize listing quality to improve CVR.

4. Customer Satisfaction (15% weight): Reviews, ratings, return rate, A-to-Z claims. A 4.5★ product with 500 reviews will outrank a 4.0★ product with 100 reviews, all else being equal.

Beginner's Ad Strategy — First 90 Days

Week 1–2: Discovery Phase — Launch 2 Auto campaigns (one for hero product, one for #2 product). Set conservative bids (₹5–8). Budget: ₹500/day. Goal: let Amazon find which keywords and ASINs convert for you. This is your keyword "harvest."

Week 3–4: Manual Migration — Download search term report. Find keywords with 3+ conversions and ACOS < 25%. Move these to Manual Exact Match campaigns with higher bids (₹10–15). Negate them from Auto campaigns to avoid double-spending.

Month 2: Expand — Add Phrase Match campaigns for broader discovery. Launch competitor targeting — target ASINs of top 5 competitors with Sponsored Products. Start your first Sponsored Brands campaign (requires Brand Registry).

Month 3+: Layered Strategy

Defense: Bid on your own brand keywords (₹3–5 bid, ACOS will be 5–10%). Prevents competitors from stealing your branded searches.

Offense: Target competitor ASINs and brand keywords with higher bids. Expect higher ACOS (25–35%) but you're stealing market share.

Expansion: Category targeting, Sponsored Display retargeting, Sponsored Brands Video (highest CVR ad type on Amazon).

Lightning Deals

What: Time-bound flash deals (4–12 hours) that appear on Amazon's Deals page. Massive traffic boost.

Cost: ₹2,500–₹10,000 per deal slot (more during festive). Plus, you offer 15–25% discount on MRP.

Eligibility: 3+ star rating, Prime-eligible, sufficient inventory, competitive pricing.

Best timing: Monday 10 AM or Friday 6 PM slots get highest traffic. During GIF/BBD, submit 4+ weeks in advance — slots run out fast.

Deal ROI Calculation

Formula: Deal ROI = (Incremental Units × Margin per Unit) - Deal Fee Example (Hair Oil ₹399 MRP, 20% deal discount): Deal Price: ₹319 Your COGS: ₹120 Platform Fees (~15%): ₹48 Margin per deal unit: ₹319 - ₹120 - ₹48 = ₹151 Normal daily sales: 40 units Deal day sales: 180 units Incremental units: 140 Incremental profit: 140 × ₹151 = ₹21,140 Deal fee: ₹5,000 NET ROI: ₹21,140 - ₹5,000 = ₹16,140 ✅ PROFITABLE PLUS: Rank boost + review velocity + BSR improvement = compounding returns

G Scaling Strategies — 20-Year Veteran Playbook

▼
STRATEGY #01

The Subscribe & Save Flywheel

S&S customers reorder automatically every 1–6 months. They churn less, have higher LTV, and Amazon gives S&S products organic rank boost. Offer 5–10% S&S discount. Target: 15% of revenue from S&S within 6 months. For FMCG brands, this is a goldmine — I've seen brands get 25%+ revenue from S&S alone.

STRATEGY #02

The Variant Expansion Play

Once your hero product hits 100+ reviews and 8%+ CVR, launch size variants (100ml, 200ml, 500ml) and flavor/type variants. This dominates more search real estate, captures different price points, and builds a "product family" that dominates the category page. Each variant inherits parent ASIN review velocity.

STRATEGY #03

The Review Velocity Strategy

First 30 reviews: Use Amazon Vine (free product, Amazon selects reviewers). Next 70: Insert a card inside the packaging saying "Love our product? Share your experience on Amazon." + follow up with Amazon's built-in "Request a Review" button 7 days after delivery. Target: 100 reviews in 60 days. Never, EVER buy fake reviews.

STRATEGY #04

ASIN Clustering Tactic

Group related ASINs under parent-child variations. This consolidates reviews, improves discoverability, and dominates search results. A parent ASIN with 5 child variants and 500+ combined reviews crushes a single ASIN with 100 reviews in organic ranking.

STRATEGY #05

Competitor Conquesting

Target top 10 competitor ASINs with Sponsored Display ads. When someone views a competitor's product, they see YOUR ad. Expect 25–40% ACOS (higher than search ads) but you're stealing customers at the moment of highest intent. This is offense. Play it aggressively during festive season.

STRATEGY #06

Seasonal Inventory Pre-positioning

GIF/BBD traffic = 5–8x normal. If you stock-out on Day 2, you've lost 70% of the event revenue. Plan inventory 90 days before peak: forecast 3x normal demand, ship to FBA 30 days early (to clear receiving delays), keep 30% buffer at your warehouse for emergency replenishment.

STRATEGY #07

The Bundle Play

Create virtual bundles (Amazon's feature) or physical combo packs. "Hair Oil + Shampoo + Serum" bundle at ₹999 vs ₹1,200 individual. This increases AOV by 30-50%, creates a unique ASIN with no competition, and customers perceive higher value. Works brilliantly in BPC and FMCG.

STRATEGY #08

A+ to Premium A+ Upgrade

Standard A+ content is free. Premium A+ (interactive modules, video, Q&A) is available to brands with Brand Story published. I've seen Premium A+ lift CVR by 5–10% consistently. That's ₹10L+ incremental monthly revenue for a ₹1Cr/month brand. The effort-to-impact ratio is extraordinary.

STRATEGY #09

Lightning Deal Stacking

During GIF, submit deals for 3–4 SKUs across different days. Day 1: Hero product deals (generates traffic). Day 3: Cross-sell products (captures returning customers). Day 5: Bundle deals (increases AOV). Each deal boosts BSR, which improves organic rank for weeks after.

STRATEGY #10

Brand Store Traffic Play

Amazon Brand Store is your mini D2C site within Amazon. Drive external traffic (Instagram, YouTube influencers) to your Brand Store using Amazon Attribution links. Benefits: you get Brand Referral Bonus (10% cashback on referral fees), zero competition on the page, and higher conversion because it's a curated experience.

Flipkart Listing vs Amazon Listing — Key Differences

DO NOT copy-paste your Amazon listing to Flipkart. I've seen this mistake destroy Flipkart potential for dozens of brands. Here's what's different:

Title Structure: Flipkart titles are shorter (max 140 chars vs Amazon's 200). Structure: Brand + Product + Key Attribute + Size. Less keyword stuffing, more clarity. Flipkart's search algorithm weights title differently — clarity > keyword density.

Flipkart-Specific Content Tips

H Meesho P&L Analysis — Unit Economics

▼

Understanding Meesho unit economics is critical because the margin structure is fundamentally different from Amazon/Flipkart:

MEESHO UNIT ECONOMICS — Hair Oil 100ml (₹199 MRP) Revenue per unit: ₹199 Platform Commission: ₹0 (ZERO — Meesho's advantage) Logistics (Valmo): -₹35 (varies by weight/distance) Packaging: -₹12 COGS: -₹65 Payment Gateway: -₹4 (~2% of selling price) ───────────────────────────────────────── GROSS MARGIN per unit: ₹83 (41.7%) Now factor in Returns + RTO: Return rate: 15% → cost per return ₹35 (reverse logistics) RTO rate: 10% → cost per RTO ₹70 (both-way shipping) Effective loss per 100 units: 15 × ₹35 + 10 × ₹70 = ₹1,225 Per-unit blended impact: -₹12.25 Ad Spend (₹3 CPC, 2% conversion): -₹150 per order = -₹150 ad cost per sale? Actually: ₹3 CPC × 50 clicks per sale = -₹150? No. Better metric: TACOS 8% = ₹16 per unit ───────────────────────────────────────── NET MARGIN per unit: ₹54.75 (27.5%) vs Amazon Net Margin for same product: ₹38 (19%) — because Amazon takes 12% referral fee MEESHO WINS on unit economics for value products. The catch: you need 3-4x the volume to match Amazon revenue.

Veteran insight: Meesho's zero commission is transformative for brands with COGS under 40% of MRP. If your COGS is above 50% of MRP, the return/RTO costs eat your advantage. Know your numbers before committing resources.
